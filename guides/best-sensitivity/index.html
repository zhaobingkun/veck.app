Veck.io Best Sensitivity Baseline

There is no universal perfect sensitivity. There is only a baseline that matches your movement speed, your screen space, and your ability to stop on target without panic corrections.

Last reviewed April 7, 2026Notes from the veck.app editor

Start with control, not speed

If you can place the crosshair calmly but struggle to turn, you are close. If every correction is a snap and overshoot, you are too high.

Test three motions

  • Short flick to a nearby angle
  • Medium sweep across a doorway
  • Tracking while strafing

A good baseline feels acceptable in all three. It does not need to feel amazing in only one.

Use a two-step tuning rule

Adjust in small steps, then play real matches. Do not evaluate based on aim drills alone. Real fights expose overreactions, panic turns, and poor stopping control.

Keep the baseline for enough games

Use the same sensitivity for at least five to ten sessions before judging it. Your hands need repetition more than novelty.
