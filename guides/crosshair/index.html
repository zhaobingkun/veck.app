Veck.io Crosshair Guide

A crosshair should help you see the shot, not distract you from it. Good crosshair tuning is mostly about visibility and consistency, not style.

Last reviewed April 7, 2026Notes from the veck.app editor

Start with visibility

If the crosshair disappears into the map, it is wrong for you. The first job of a crosshair is to stay visible without pulling too much attention.

Thickness matters

The current settings snapshot uses a thickness of 3.60. That may be a usable baseline if you want something clearly visible, but the real test is whether it hides targets or helps center them.

Do not retune every session

Players often blame the crosshair for misses caused by movement timing or bad route choices. Keep the crosshair stable while you test aim and positioning habits.
